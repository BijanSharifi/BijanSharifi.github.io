About me

I'm a rising junior at The University of Maryland College Park studying computer science. I'm currently pursuing a programming internship of some form for summer 2024. My main areas of interest are A.I development and bioinformatics. I landed in college park in the fall of 2021 with no intentions of studying computer science, but I decided to take CMSC 131, which is the first of the core CS classes at UMD. I knew that computer science was the future of our world, and I wanted to give it a shot and maybe learn how to program. As the class progressed, it was a struggle. Programming background was not "required" but it sure seemed like everybody in the class knew what they were doing and I felt lost. I would work for hours on very basic tasks/concepts but I would still feel lost. I was getting bad grades on the midterms and my confidence was wavering. But my hard work never waivered and I slowly started to climb up that learning curve. I still remember the moment when certain concepts started clicking. Looping through and array and assigning the different indices with some value was what I was so proud to understand. At the time it was such a big deal to me. Now I look back and laugh at those times where I was struggling with something that feels so basic now. And in the future, I know I will look back on this time and laugh at how little I knew. I still have so much to learn, and I am willing to work for it. I am eager to receive a position for summer 2024. With the amount I have learned in just the past 2 years at school, I can only imagine how much more I can and will learn. Outside computer science/academics, I enjoy health and fitness, basketball, as well as spending quality time with family and friends. I am also a member of The Baha'i Faith, which has given me opportunities to help serve and take leadership roles in community building activities in the pursuit of a more unified planet.
